Rob — Finding Waves

By Rob • February 26, 2026

Rob was introduced to surfing through Open Ocean Collective. Born with cerebral palsy, he had long believed the sea and surf were out of reach. With patient volunteers, adaptive coaching and a community that emphasises welcome and safety, Rob discovered not only how to catch a wave but how empowering the ocean can be.

"I remember the first time I felt the board move beneath me — it was like flying," Rob says. "The community helped me find new confidence and taught me that adapting doesn't mean giving up; it means finding new ways to be part of the waves."

Rob now joins group surf sessions whenever possible and helps mentor new participants who need extra support. His story is a reminder that surfing can be made accessible to many bodies and that inclusive communities create space for everyone to thrive.

Maya — From Fear to Freedom

By Maya • February 20, 2026

I was terrified of the ocean. Growing up, I never imagined myself as a surfer. But when I found Open Ocean Collective, everything changed. The community welcomed me without judgment, and my first instructors were incredibly patient and encouraging.

The waves taught me so much more than just how to paddle and pop up. They taught me resilience, courage, and self-belief. Every time I face a challenge on the water, I think about all the challenges I've overcome off it. Surfing became my therapy, my happy place, my escape.

Now I'm the one encouraging others to give it a try. If I can do it, anyone can. The ocean has given me a community, a passion, and a piece of myself I didn't know was missing.

Ahmed — Breaking Barriers

By Ahmed • February 15, 2026

As a young Muslim man from east London, I never saw people who looked like me out in the water. Surfing seemed like something for someone else, someone different. When I discovered Open Ocean Collective, I finally felt like I belonged.

The diversity in the lineup is what drew me in and kept me coming back. Seeing women, people of color, people from different backgrounds all sharing a passion for the ocean broke down so many stereotypes I didn't even know I had.

Now, surfing is my favorite way to spend a Saturday morning. The water doesn't care about your background, your beliefs, or where you come from. It only cares that you show up ready to learn and have fun. Open Ocean Collective gave me that freedom.
